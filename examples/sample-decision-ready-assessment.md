# Sample Decision-Ready Infrastructure Assessment

## Objective
Identify and rank the most justifiable intervention zones for a regional digital infrastructure program.

## Executive recommendation
Prioritise Zone B first, Zone A second, and hold Zone C as a monitored reserve option.

## Why this is the recommendation
### Zone B — highest priority
- strongest combination of service gap severity and population relevance
- viable intervention scale
- best fit with near-term funding justification
- clearest evidence package for decision-makers

### Zone A — second priority
- genuine need exists
- economically rational under a broader resilience framing
- slightly weaker immediate intervention case than Zone B

### Zone C — watch, not act first
- some indicators are positive
- current evidence does not justify first-round intervention ahead of Zones A and B

## Ranked assessment summary
| Zone | Priority | Relative case strength | Recommended action |
|------|----------|------------------------|--------------------|
| Zone B | 1 | Strong | Proceed to detailed business case |
| Zone A | 2 | Moderate-strong | Hold as second-wave option |
| Zone C | 3 | Moderate | Monitor and revisit when conditions change |

## Evidence considered
- service gap severity
- population and user relevance
- resilience importance
- delivery feasibility
- likely funding defensibility

## Constraints and caveats
- this is a prioritisation output, not final engineering design
- final delivery sequencing still depends on field validation and commercial assumptions
- evidence quality is sufficient for shortlist decisions, not yet for procurement specification

## What the client receives
- ranked shortlist
- rationale for each zone
- decision-ready recommendation
- evidence summary suitable for executive review
- next-step action path

## Next step
Approve Zone B for detailed business-case development and retain Zone A as the second-wave candidate.
